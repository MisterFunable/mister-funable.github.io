## Part 6: Vento Aureo

This had more to do with the borders. Since my last prompt asked to move the head more to the top, the upper border will be small, which I'm fine with.

<!-- Image Source: https://miro.medium.com/1*JH9UaFDijIlxfUui4xNB7g.png | Local: images/24/img-32.png -->
![](images/24/img-32.png)

<!-- Image Source: https://miro.medium.com/1*__W6yB4LYtThhO-TtMwoEQ.jpeg | Local: images/24/img-33.jpg -->
![It doesn't bother me, It bothers me, it bothers me a lot.](images/24/img-33.jpg)

The issue with the border colors was noticeable if you added it to pages that didn't allow you to adjust the position of the image. For those cases, you would see white/empty space.

So to avoid that stupid problem, I:

1. Locally removed the background with the Image Preview from macOS.

2. Upscaled it with [Upscale Media](https://www.upscale.media/es).

3. Shared it to my phone.

4. Opened Lightroom, adjusted colors, removed the thingy from the circle.

5. Shared it back to my computer.

6. Edited it on Canva to add the background.

And finally it was ready to share on social media. And since it was Friday, with this, it's more than enough.

## What's Next

If there's something to take from this, it's that prompting until you hit gold is inefficient. Stopping to ask yourself "what do I actually want?" saves time. And if you're not on a free tier burning through credits, maybe sketch something first.

That said, editing skills and knowing the right tools go a long way. The AI got me 80% there, but Canva, Lightroom, and macOS Image Preview handled the rest.

You don't need to be a designer, but knowing how to remove a background or adjust colors makes a difference.

Next post (maybe not from this series) will be about organization. Keeping your online presence consistent requires planning, and things scale fast. Especially when you're juggling multiple accounts and activities. But that will be for another time.
